Il Progetto

This website and its contents are the outcome results of a collaborative project between a group of researchers coming from several fields: ontology, surveying and computer science.

The project leader is the 3DOM laboratory, part of the Fondazione Bruno Kessler, and it was developed under an European Marie Curie Project called ITN-DCH (Initial Training Network for Digital Cultural Heritage).

The main goal was to obtain the fully 3D documentation of the external part of the castle, as well as several internal spaces decorated with frescoes. The digitalization was performed using terrestrial photogrammetry. The newest one was referenced and scaled to a previously available point cloud, produced by FBK during an aerial image acquisition .

All the produced data is now free to be explored in the website, and in order to allow other professionals to critically assess our work, we additionally enriched the produced data with a series of semantic information with describe the acquisition process.

Last but not least, the authors would like to thank all the personnel of Buonconsiglio castle in Trento (Italy). In particular we wish to acknowledge Silvano Zamboni, Adriano Conci and Matteo Rapanà for their kindness and support during the data collection days.
